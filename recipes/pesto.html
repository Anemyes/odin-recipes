Description

Pesto, or Pesto alla Genovese, is a sauce originating in Genoa, the capital city of Liguria, Italy. It traditionally consists of crushed garlic, European pine nuts, coarse salt, basil leaves, and hard cheese such as Parmigiano-Reggiano or Pecorino Sardo, all blended with olive oil.

Ingredients

  • 2 garlic, or more to taste
  • 2 tablespoons pine nuts
  • 1 bunch fresh basil leaves
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese
  • 1 1/2 tablespoons grated Pecorino romano cheese
  • 1/2 cup extra-virgin olive oil

Steps

  1. Crush garlic using a mortar and pestle
  2. Add pine nuts; crush with the garlic
  3. Add basil leaves gradually, making circular movements with the pestle, until a smooth paste forms
  4. Mix Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese and Pecorino romano chesse using a wooden spoon
  5. Stir in olive oil until pesto is blend

Notes

If you don't have a pestle and mortar, you can also use a food processor or blender. Chill the blade and container in the fridge for 1 hour and the process in pulses to avoid heating the pesto mixture. Add basil, pine nuts, and garlic in 3 batches, then follow with the cheese and the oil as above.
